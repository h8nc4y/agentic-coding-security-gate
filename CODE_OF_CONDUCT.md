# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We want participation in `agentic-coding-security-gate` to be safe, respectful,
and constructive for everyone. Contributors, maintainers, and users are expected
to help create an environment free from harassment and intimidation, regardless
of identity, background, experience level, or point of view.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, specific, and patient when discussing security-sensitive work.
- Critique ideas and changes rather than people.
- Prefer minimal, synthetic reproductions and evidence-backed claims.
- Respect privacy, consent, and the repository's approval gates.
- Accept correction, acknowledge mistakes, and help repair their impact.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, discriminatory language, or unwanted sexual attention.
- Personal attacks, deliberate disruption, or sustained bad-faith argument.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Pressuring anyone to reveal credentials, private data, or protected reports.
- Retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith.

## Scope

This code applies in repository spaces and whenever someone represents this
project in public or private project-related communication.

## Reporting and Enforcement

For conduct concerns, use the private-first reporting path in
[`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md). Do not put sensitive details, personal data, raw
logs, screenshots, or credentials in a public issue. If no private channel is
available, publish only a minimal synthetic summary and ask the maintainer for a
private channel.

Maintainers will review reports as promptly and impartially as practical, limit
disclosure to people who need the information, and recuse themselves when they
have a conflict of interest. Depending on severity and history, a response may
include a private correction, warning, temporary participation restriction, or
permanent removal from project spaces. Knowingly false or retaliatory reports
may also lead to corrective action.
